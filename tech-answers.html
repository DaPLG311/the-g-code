Most Asked

The questions everyone starts with.

Do I need to know how to code?

No. You understand the business, audience, mission, or problem — we translate that into the technical plan.

What's the difference between a website and an app?

A website mainly explains, displays, or converts. An app lets users perform ongoing actions, manage information, or operate a system. Some projects combine both.

Why not build every feature immediately?

Every extra feature adds cost, time, testing, and risk. The MVP identifies the smallest version that creates value and proves what should happen next.

Will I own my product?

Ownership, source-code access, licensed tools, third-party services, and handoff terms are stated clearly in the project agreement.

Can AI build the entire product by itself?

AI accelerates research, design, coding, testing, and content. Human judgment, direction, security, and accountability remain necessary.

What happens after launch?

The product enters a measurement and improvement stage — users, conversion, feedback, errors, and priorities are reviewed before the next build.

Are advertising costs included?

Advertising spend is separate unless the proposal specifically includes it.

Can Day One MVP™ create the business too?

Yes — the customer, offer, identity, revenue model, pricing, product, sales path, marketing, systems, media, and launch plan.

Can you train my employees?

Yes — AI, sales, onboarding, workflows, tools, systems, media, or custom company programs.

Do you work with nonprofits?

Yes — operations, outreach, fundraising infrastructure, training, volunteer systems, education, and mission delivery.
